# Context: Fantasy Ship Game

Single-context repo. This glossary states what each term **is**; the why behind each lives in the ADR or issue it cites — read that before re-litigating a decision. See `docs/adr/`.

## Glossary

### Engine core (headless/UI boundary)

- **Sim** — the single mutable core simulation state for a voyage: ships, crew, combat, RNG. No dependency on rendering or input devices; runs identically headless or under a UI.
  _Avoid_: game state, world.
- **Command** — an operation submitted to the Sim that mutates its state. The only way presentation is allowed to affect the Sim.
  _Avoid_: action, input.
- **Event** — a fact emitted by the Sim describing something that happened (e.g. `DamageDealt`, `AwaitingCaptainDecision`). The only way presentation learns what happened inside the Sim.
  _Avoid_: message, notification.
- **Tick** — one call to the Sim's step procedure: resolves an entire round of auto-battler logic instantly and batch-emits that round's Events. A logical sim step, not tied to wall-clock time.
  _Avoid_: frame; step (when referring to rendering).
- **run_session** — the single driver loop, shared by headless and UI: calls Tick, dispatches Events to an Event_Sink, and asks an Input_Source for a captain's decision whenever the Sim is awaiting one.
- **Input_Source** — the pluggable source `run_session` asks for a captain's decision. Headless: scripted or seeded-random; UI: renders a decision menu and blocks until the player picks.
- **Event_Sink** — the pluggable destination `run_session` dispatches a Tick's Events to. Headless: logs or records; UI: plays back with animation, blocking until playback finishes.
- **Headless mode** — running a session with no rendering — tests, simulation, ghost-battle resolution. A separate executable, compile-time incapable of importing the rendering library.
  _Avoid_: simulation mode, test mode.
- **UI mode** — running a session with a human player and real-time rendering.
  _Avoid_: game mode, client mode.

### Ship & crew model (the weight economy — ADR-0020)

**The weight economy**, stated once (ADR-0020; entries below assume it): a ship's wealth **is** the cargo stowed in its holds — no money, no purse field — cargo weighs its contents 1:1 on whatever holds it, and Speed is **derived, never authored**: `speed = base + modifiers − weight/10`.

- **Ship** — a voyage's vessel: Hull, Max Hull, a Speed *calibration* `base`, and a fixed **layout**. Combat power, weight, and cargo all come from what's installed in the layout, not from the ship directly.
- **Hull** — the ship's health, persisting across the entire voyage. 0 sinks the ship and ends the voyage (permadeath). Restored only by **Repair** (the Brace verb — ADR-0027 — and a Trade's Hull gain), never above **Max Hull**. ADR-0006.
  _Avoid_: **Durability** — flat per-hit damage reduction, deleted (#396, ADR-0026); damage lands whole and Hull is the only thing between a hit and a sunk ship.
- **Speed** — governs disengagement: once a battle passes its baseline round count, the higher-Speed ship may Break Off; also breaks mutual-kill and round-cap ties. A derived `int` (escape gates and comparisons need integer comparison). `base` is one calibration constant (whatever makes the starting ship read `STARTING_SPEED :: 4`), so Speed varies with **what the ship carries**, not between ships. Weight is a subtrahend, not a replacement: `Modify_Speed` items and Trade axes still land in `modifiers`. **Floors at 0** as an authoring invariant, asserted (`base − weight/10 >= 0`), never a live clamp — 0 means *too laden to run*, reached by getting rich. ADR-0006 (amended), ADR-0020.
  _Avoid_: initiative, turn order (combat resolves simultaneously by phase); an authored/per-ship Speed.
- **Weight** — what Speed is read against: `weight/10` is subtracted, so the divisor is the exchange rate between cargo and Speed (10 is forced — coarser makes a Small hold's jettison buy 0 Speed). A fitting weighs `weight + cargo_held` — its authored mass plus the cargo stowed in it, one sum. Guns are permanently heavy, cargo only while stowed: **emptiness, not loadout, is what varies a ship's Speed.** Same `int` units as cargo; deliberately not `distinct` — "cargo is weight" is true at the value level (ADR-0011, ADR-0020).
  _Avoid_: mass, tonnage; and weight for **bulk** — weight is *mass*, bulk is *volume*.
- **Bulk** — the **volume a fitting's own machinery takes inside its slot** (`Fitting.bulk`): `capacity = contribution − bulk`, derived, never stored. An ordinary item authors its full contribution and carries nothing; a **hold** authors 0 and carries the whole slot. Zero is the carrying end, so every authored fitting names it; `roster_item`'s `bulk` parameter defaults to the size's full contribution (`the_roster_carries_nothing` guards overrides). Bulk is what buys **jettison granularity**: part-full fittings shed finer than one big hold. #399; its budget cost is #384's.
  _Avoid_: bulk as a synonym for weight or for slot size — neither the mass nor the container.
- **Layout** — the fixed set of **slots** a ship template defines. No grid/coordinates, no shape packing — discrete, sized, named containers.
- **Slot** — a single unit of layout: a **size** (small / medium / large) and a **base visibility** (exposed / concealed). Slot names are flavor only.
- **Fitting** — the single, unified concept for anything occupying a slot: crew, weapons, holds, creatures, magical objects — deliberately not parallel type systems. Field set **closed** (#407) at `name, size, weight, bulk, cargo_held, tags, requires_exposed, effects, effect_count` (up to three effects, cap in the type — ADR-0029). No visibility of its own (ADR-0030) and no category — the phase rides on each effect, so one fitting may feed both phases. No special-cased cargo kind (#399): carrying is an axis every fitting sits on. **POD with exactly one mutable instance field, `cargo_held`** — everything else authored, which is what lets selectors read tags without live state and keeps a ghost snapshot's one-level copy sound. The stronger "immutable POD" claim is false; build nothing on it.
  _Avoid_: `category`, `visibility_override`, `passive`/`active`, the `Condition` union — all deleted (#405, #407).
- **Tag family** — one of five (Crew, Weapon, Beast, Artifact, Cargo). A fitting carries a *set* of them, usually one (multi-tag allowed, used sparingly). The axis synergy effects count along; drives no behavior alone. **`Cargo` means "this fitting's job is carrying", not "this fitting is carrying"** — authored, never derived from `cargo_held` (a derived tag would be a selector reading live state).
- **Cargo** — the ship's wealth: the one thing you carry, worth something only in trade. An **axis, not a kind of fitting** (#399): anything with bulk to spare can hold some, so a gun can also carry. Contributes to no combat phase; it is what you spend, what a Reward fills, what a wreck pays out. ADR-0004, ADR-0020, #399.
  _Avoid_: "a cargo fitting" as a kind; "stack"; money / treasure / purse.
- **Hold** — a real installable fitting: the degenerate corner of the cargo axis — zero bulk, zero mass, no effects, Cargo-tagged — minted by `ship_fitting_hold` at S/M/L. **Free**: never a `Roster_Item`, no tier by construction, costs nothing, never in a shop (the roster stays at exactly 50). Every unclaimed slot carries one, and **removing a fitting backfills one**, so an empty slot is never managed. "Your hold is full" reads collectively. #399.
  _Avoid_: "cargo filler" and the runtime-minted cargo fitting — deleted; a hold is installed, not conjured.
- **Cargo capacity** — the ceiling on cargo: summed over **installed fittings**, each contributing its size contribution less its bulk (S 10 / M 20 / L 40). The starting 8-slot hull reads **90**. **An empty slot carries nothing** — forced, else a free zero-bulk hold is byte-identical to an empty slot — so an empty slot is wasted, not neutral (hence the starting holds and removal backfill). The ceiling is structural: overflow above capacity is **lost** (#157; free re-stowing outside battle makes that fair). Derived by `ship_cargo_capacity`, never stored; `Ship.base_cargo_capacity` / `Captain.cargo_capacity_bonus` are deleted. ADR-0020, #157, #399.
  _Avoid_: a baseline capacity stat, a stored number, "empty slots count".
- **Stowage** — how a cargo total lands in the fittings that can hold it (`ship_stow_cargo(layout, amount)`): **water-filling** — equal shares poured over everything with capacity, capped at each room, unused share cascading; odd units to the largest remaining room, ties to the lower slot index. A **pure function of `(amount, capacities present)`**, so every caller passes a scalar total and re-derives the arrangement. Granularity is a property of the **build**, not of how little you carry. #399.
  _Avoid_: smallest-slot-first — retired with the granularity property it encoded.
- **Fit rule** — a fitting may occupy a slot only if it matches the slot's size **exactly** (ADR-0004; see it for the flagged extension point) **and** the slot is exposed whenever the fitting requires exposure (ADR-0030). Two gates, stated once in `ship_fitting_fits`, so install, replace and move admit the same fittings.
- **Visibility** — a fitting's visibility is **the visibility of the slot it sits in**, full stop: what scouting shows, what `Selector(Visibility)` counts, what `Own_Visibility` reads. No resolution step, no `ship_effective_visibility`. ADR-0030.
  _Avoid_: **effective visibility** and the three-layer precedence chain (ADR-0005) — an override let an item take a concealed reading out of an exposed slot for free, capacity the power budget cannot see.
- **Requires exposed** — `Fitting.requires_exposed`, checked at fit-legality beside the size gate: an item that only works in the open cannot install below deck, and costs its captain one of four exposed slots, contested against the guns. A `bool`, not a `Maybe(Visibility)` — widening later is free, and "requires concealed" asks for nothing where concealment is the cheaper half. Authored through `roster_item`; no roster item uses it yet. ADR-0030.
- **Captain** — a voyage-start choice, structurally separate from the slot system (not a fitting, consumes no slot). Can influence slot limits/structure and grants additional manual per-round captain actions. Names part of the starting cargo: `Captain.starting_cargo_bonus` (`CAPTAIN_STARTING_CARGO :: 10` for Odessa) — a *fillable* lever, unlike the deleted `cargo_capacity_bonus`. Starting cargo is `STARTING_CARGO :: 40` + that bonus, water-filled by `ship_stow_cargo`; "a full hold" is derived from the sum, not a constant.

### Combat resolution (ADR-0006, amended by ADR-0017, ADR-0025, ADR-0028)

- **Round** — one unit of battle resolution: the captain's decision is applied, then fitting effects resolve through Brace → Fire. Corresponds to one Tick.
- **Phase** — one of the two fixed stages (Brace, Fire), in that order. Both ships resolve a phase together (simultaneous, never sequential-by-Speed); a ship's own fittings trigger in fixed slot order. **Every effect names the phase that consumes it** (ADR-0029); **a phase has one verb** — Brace repairs, Fire deals damage (`ship_verb_phase`) — and the `effect_*` helpers set phase from verb so the two cannot disagree (a roster test guards hand-built literals).
  _Avoid_: **Muster** — the third phase, removed #314 (ADR-0025); its fittings are Fire now, and "crew vs guns" rides on Tag families.
- **Brace** — the phase resolving **repair**: pressed Brace output restores own Hull, applied **before any Fire output lands**, so a repair can save a captain the round they would otherwise sink — that ordering is why Brace is a phase, and the death check is its one consumer. Fills the gap to Max Hull, never more (repair into a full hull is a silent no-op). Both phases are **totalled before either writes**, so a Hull-gated fitting reads the hull its captain saw. ADR-0027.
  _Avoid_: Brace as damage reduction — nothing is subtracted from a hit (ADR-0026); site-scaled repair — an unkillable hostile (the zero-damage floor ADR-0019 barred).
- **Fire** — the phase resolving the round's damage: a side's pressed Fire output *is* its raw damage. Holds guns and the former crew (ex-Muster) fittings. ADR-0025.
- **Raw damage** — a side's pressed Fire output, and the *whole* of what lands: `final_damage = raw`, nothing subtracted, no floor to clamp. ADR-0026.
  _Avoid_: **bulwark** (formerly *soak*) — the subtracted term, deleted #396.
- **Order** — what a captain submits for a round, one per round, from a closed set of exactly five: **Press / Commit / Jettison / Break Off / Hold** (ADR-0028). All plain data; the set costs a ghost snapshot no state beyond Press's spent flag. The **readable** order set is narrower: Jettison is excluded (#400 — a once-a-voyage panic is not a choice to reward), and nothing reads any order yet (`Battle_State` carries the round and the two speeds only).
  _Avoid_: **Man the Sails** and **Reallocate** — both deleted #398.
- **Press** — multiplies **its own phase's fittings** by `PRESS_MULTIPLIER :: 3`, this round only, **at most once per battle** (tracked by `combat_may_press`; a second submit in one fight is a driver bug, asserted). Targets Brace or Fire; both arms read. The ration is the point: it asks *when*, not *whether*. ADR-0017, ADR-0028.
- **Commit** — Press's every-round sibling that pays for itself: **×2 Brace, Fire to zero** (`COMMIT_MULTIPLIER :: 2`). **One-directional by construction** — the mirror would strictly dominate — and since a committing captain deals no damage, Commit can never *win* a fight, only survive one. ADR-0028.
  _Avoid_: a symmetric Commit; a rationed one — the sacrifice is what pays for every-round availability.
- **Break Off** — ends the battle immediately for both ships without destruction; available once a ship's Speed exceeds its opponent's after the baseline round count. Read off Speed alone — nothing in a battle grants Speed any more. ADR-0006, ADR-0028.
- **Hold** — the stance of taking no other order: behaviourally a no-op but a **named, player-selectable order** and a fact an item can gate on — never a nil command (nil encodes "the driver submitted nothing"). A scripted ship submits it every round it isn't auto-taking Break Off. ADR-0028.
  _Avoid_: nil as "the captain held"; "Hold" for a lull in the fight.
- **Jettison Cargo** — a captain decision that **empties one fitting's cargo, re-stows the remainder, and emits the event** — nothing more. The fitting stays in its slot (#399): the cargo goes over the side, not the thing holding it. The Speed gain is the weight drop itself (ADR-0020). Jettisoned cargo is **always destroyed**, never claimed as spoils. Cannot heave an empty fitting (`combat_apply_jettison` asserts `cargo_held > 0`). The re-stow (#400, same water-fill as every cargo change) makes jettison **self-flattening**: each heave sheds no more than the last, so the escape window closes as it is used. One order that names its target in a second step (the fight menu's row becomes the laden fittings; that step *is* the confirmation). Excluded from the readable order set. **One act on two surfaces, priced differently** (#401): under fire it is an order and costs the round; **at anchor it is free and repeatable** — a drag onto the hold ledger, confirmed, never the discard bin (which means *this thing leaves the ship*). Both surfaces go through `ship_jettison_cargo`. Accepted: weight is a free precision dial at anchor; authored granularity binds only in battle. ADR-0006 (amended), ADR-0020, ADR-0028, #399–#401.
  _Avoid_: a permanent Speed bonus or jettison spoils — the Speed is the weight, the cargo is gone; "Jettison" for putting a *fitting* off the ship — the Build surface's discard bin reads *Over the Side*.
- **Mast configuration** — the **cosmetic read-out** of a ship's Speed, never an input: the masts show what the weight already decided. Speed **0** renders as **"sails in"** — a mainline reading, reached by getting rich, not an edge case. ADR-0020, #175.
  _Avoid_: mast as a Speed input or stat.

### Above a voyage (issue #278; loop architecture ADR-0022)

- **Chart Table** — the one screen above a voyage, and the whole of the game outside one. The exe boots into it; every voyage launches from it. Holds a title, **Begin a voyage**, and **Quit** — nothing else — over the chart as menu background. **Stateless**, so the start menu and the return screen are the same screen, identical at boot and after a sinking (a frame-0 screenshot is an honest photograph). "Chart" deliberately overlaps the voyage's node graph: at the Chart Table there is no chart yet, and beginning a voyage is what draws one (see #278 for why the harbour words were unavailable). Meta-progression would attach here if it ever exists — a signpost, not a promise.
  _Avoid_: start menu, main menu, shell, title screen; a stateful Chart Table; a second screen above a voyage.
- **The end-of-voyage beat belongs to the voyage, not the Chart Table** — both endings (Haven and permadeath) are told by the voyage, which owns the state that makes them mean anything, then hands back to an unchanged Chart Table.

### Voyage UI spine (in-voyage presentation — ADR-0024)

The in-voyage UI is **two top-level modes** — Home and Encounter — and nothing else, sitting above `run_session` (ADR-0022).

- **Home** — the mode at anchor, between encounters, while the Sim awaits the next travel choice. The **Build surface** is its persistent editable ground; the **chart** is a raisable overlay. The one place the player is free: refit freely, and sail.
  _Avoid_: "the refit screen" / "the travel screen" — Home is one mode holding both.
- **Encounter** (mode) — walking a node's stage list: a focused one-stage-at-a-time takeover, entered by sailing into a node, left when the walk completes or halts. The chart is raisable here **view-only**.
- **Build surface** — the **one** refit surface, and the whole of refit. Persistent at Home; reused by Offer and Shop stages with a **shelf** added. Available everywhere except inside a **Fight**. Every edit commits live (each install/move/remove is a command emitting an Event); there is no inventory.
  _Avoid_: a modal refit per Offer/Shop; a separate placement UI — placement *is* refit.
- **The flick** — the mouse drag (press–drag–release) that raises and lowers the chart overlay, anchored by a corner tab or screen edge; a click on the tab is an acceptable first-build stand-in. Swipe-raisable everywhere, **sailable only at Home** — mid-encounter its nodes are greyed.
  _Avoid_: arrow keys or a screen-switch — the flick lays a second live surface over the first.
- **The shelf** — the temporary source container an Offer or Shop stage adds beside the ship's slots: the items on offer / the stock. Taking one is an ordinary refit move; only a Shop's shelf→ship transfer costs cargo. Stage-scoped.
  _Avoid_: an inventory — not a store carried between nodes.
- **Playback layer** — the single shared, blocking beat overlay any stage invokes on top of itself; every `Event_Sink` beat renders through it. A shared surface, not a change to the blocking contract (ADR-0002, ADR-0022).
  _Avoid_: each stage drawing its own beats.

### Map & voyage structure (ADR-0009, superseding ADR-0007; encounter terms ADR-0014)

- **Voyage** — one playable attempt from Start to Haven (or permadeath), launched from the Chart Table and returning to it. Not resumable/saved; save/resume and meta-progression are unspecified (#278).
- **Node** — a single location in the voyage's map: a procedurally-generated connected graph, regenerated fresh each voyage (seeded, reproducible). Carries **edges** and, if an encounter, a stage list hidden until arrival unless its **first** stage reveals.
  _Avoid_: point, tile.
- **Edge** — a directed connection, generated forward only (layer *i* → *i+1*) but walkable both ways at runtime.
- **Layer** — nodes generate in ordered layers (a few wide); edges connect adjacent layers.
- **Zone** — one of three fixed **stakes** bands in fixed order: Coastal → Open Sea → The Deep. Stakes rise with zone, and zone hard-maps to stage count — Coastal 1, Open Sea 2, The Deep 3 (ADR-0014).
- **Depth** — a node's layer index within its zone, normalized. Stakes rise with depth on top of the zone gradient.
- **Stakes** — how much is on the line at a node: the single gradient `Scaling_Site` (zone tier + depth), which each primitive reads through its own constants, **as much of it as it has room for**. **Fight** reads it as a *factor* — the only primitive that multiplies (ADR-0019). **Offer** reads it as a *selection* — a tier band, never runtime scaling (ADR-0031). **Reward** reads it plainly as granted cargo. **Trade** reads the zone tier only — its constants are an exchange rate quoted in reference-fight Hull swing, and a depth step would compound faster than the tier ladder (#146, ADR-0026). **Shop** reads nothing — its gradient is the cargo the captain arrives with (ADR-0015). Renames ADR-0009's "difficulty".
  _Avoid_: difficulty — it only ever described the Fight reading.
- **Start** — the voyage's origin node; also the home port. A landmark.
- **Haven** — the destination node. Reaching it with Hull > 0 wins. No port, no encounter. A landmark.
- **Landmark** — Start or Haven: a node carrying no encounter and always fully visible. Port left this category in ADR-0014.
- **Port** — an encounter node holding the one-stage `[Shop]` recipe, placed by the Port bucket — two per zone (six) plus Start, each consuming a zone node slot. Visible because it **opens** on a Shop (the revealing stage), not because it is a landmark. A Port by two things only: **where** it is put and **what it sells** (the Chandlery pool — ADR-0015); since ADR-0016, revealing ⟺ opening on a Shop ⟺ being a Port. There is no `Node_Kind.Port` — "is this a port" is asked of its stage list.
- **Encounter** — a non-landmark node's content: an ordered list of **stages** plus a cursor, walked once on **first** arrival (no decline; route around it or walk it). Revisiting never re-triggers. Exactly one Encounter type — the interaction is the stage list, not a kind tag (ADR-0014).
- **Stage** — one step of an encounter: a **stage primitive** plus the content it was baked with. Each resolves to **completed** or **halted**.
- **Stage primitive** — one of five: **Fight, Offer, Trade, Shop, Reward**. A closed set — adding an encounter must never require adding a primitive. The primitive *is* the trait; variance comes from each stage's content roster.
  _Avoid_: encounter kind, trait.
- **Complete / Halt** — the two stage outcomes, and the whole of encounter control flow. Completed advances the cursor; halted ends the encounter, keeping what earlier stages granted. The primitive defines which is which — Fight: victory completes, Break Off halts, sinking ends the voyage; Offer: pick/skip; Trade: accept/reject; Shop: leaving completes; Reward: always completes. Recipes author **no** gates.
- **Recipe** — a named, authored stage set: the unit of encounter content (`Sea Battle = [Fight, Reward]`). Generation **picks a whole recipe**; each stage rolls content from its own roster — generation never composes a stage list. A recipe authors a **stage spec** — primitive plus what it cannot draw for itself (today only a Shop's **stock pool** — ADR-0015). The **catalog** (#138) is the fifteen recipes below and nothing else; one **shape** (kind sequence) may be authored once only.

  | Bucket | Recipes |
  | --- | --- |
  | 1 stage — Coastal | **Skirmish** `[Fight]` · **Flotsam** `[Offer]` · **Bargain** `[Trade]` · **Drifting Salvage** `[Reward]` |
  | 2 stages — Open Sea | **Sea Battle** `[Fight, Reward]` · **Derelict** `[Offer, Reward]` · **Boarding Action** `[Fight, Offer]` · **Press Gang** `[Fight, Shop]` · **Smuggler's Cove** `[Trade, Shop]` · **Privateer's Toll** `[Trade, Reward]` |
  | 3 stages — The Deep | **Contested Anchorage** `[Fight, Shop, Reward]` · **Sunken Reliquary** `[Offer, Shop, Reward]` · **Prize Convoy** `[Fight, Reward, Shop]` · **Smuggler's Run** `[Trade, Shop, Offer]` · **Kraken's Wake** `[Fight, Trade, Reward]` |
  | Port (bespoke) | **Port** `[Shop: Chandlery]` |

  Two authoring conventions, checked by test: **costs precede boons** (a halt is an exit, so declinable costs — Fight, Trade — are authored ahead of the boons they pay for), and **only the Port bucket opens on a Shop** (the map labels a revealed encounter by its first stage, so a `[Shop]` merchant would wear a Port's marker; a merchant earns its Shop by putting a stage in front of it — which is why Coastal has no shops but its two Ports).
  _Avoid_: naming a recipe after its stage tuple; a second recipe with an existing shape.
- **Bucket** — a pool of recipes plus a placement rule. The 1-/2-/3-stage buckets deal per zone via a shuffled **recipe bag**; membership is **derived** from `len(recipe.stages)`, never authored. The Port bucket places two per zone; Start and Haven are fixed.
- **Fight** — the primitive for a full battle against an opponent ship via the phased-round system (ADR-0006); mechanically identical to a future ghost-PvP battle. Draws its opponent from a **hostile roster** (#135). A Fight also **pays**: **sinking** gives the winner the wreck's hold as it stands — `voyage_finish_ship_battle` must tell `End_Reason.Destroyed` from `.Round_Cap`; a stalemate pays nothing. At the flat 50% hostile fill (#176) the payout (30–65) beats a Reward's (20–45), so overflow (#157) is the mainline result of winning.
- **Hostile archetype** — one authored entry in the Fight roster: a named build and the roster items it carries — *character*, while the node's stakes are *power* (a Deep node deals a **tougher** hostile, not a different pool). Built from the same ~50 items the player sees (ADR-0012), named, not restated. No Hull, no magnitudes, no authored Speed (derived from weight at a flat 50% hold fill — #176 placeholder). Authored at **Open Sea weight**, where the site's power factor reads 100% (ADR-0019) — met halved in Coastal, half again on top in The Deep. Item **order is authoring**: placement is first-empty-fit, exposed slots first, so a later item lands concealed — which decides whether a concealment effect fires.
  _Avoid_: treating an archetype as a difficulty rung; authoring an entry to be met undiminished by a starting ship (see ADR-0019's authoring rule, which outlives the bulwark failure mode ADR-0026 removed).
- **Hostile Speed** — the one Fight stat the stakes gradient **disowns**: the site never moves it (the 50% fill is a constant, so the property is re-derived under ADR-0020, not overturned). The player's Speed is read off their cargo, so **straddle is a joint property of (roster, player cargo)**: #135's straddle test pins the starting cargo explicitly and asserts a *point* — ≥1 hostile slower, ≥1 faster — not a window, because leaving the window as you get rich is the feature (#177). Replaces the flat constant 5 that made every hostile escape-eligible and the player never.
- **Offer** — the primitive presenting a few *distinct roster items* (or a skip) and opening a Refit to place the pick. Free. The options are **roster lines unmodified** — numbers authored, never scaled on the way in; a deeper node offers a **better item**, not a bigger number. Depth's lever is the **tier band** drawn from: top reached tier plus the one below, floor rising with ceiling (ADR-0031). Formerly the Upgrade Offer kind, repurposed by ADR-0012/#96.
  _Avoid_: an **offer bonus** — the deleted flat magnitude that made the priced line and the received item two different items.
- **Trade** — the primitive for a permanent **stat-for-stat** trade-off, both sides scaled by the node's zone, axes drawn from a roster (#136). Tradeable stats are a closed set: **Hull, Max Hull, Cargo** (Speed left #180, Durability ADR-0026). The one thing that moves Max Hull — worth buying since ADR-0027's repair fills it. A Cargo axis moves Speed as a side effect (cargo is weight) and can overflow (#157, no special case). All-or-nothing: a cost the ship can't pay in full (against the *effective* stat, floored per stat) can't be accepted — never clamped. **Open (#180):** whether a Speed-cost axis subtracts a modifier or adds ballast, and what bounds a cumulative cost once the hold fills.
- **Trade swing** — what one "unit" of trade is worth in a zone, making every trade one swing for one swing off one table. Rows quote in **reference-fight Hull swing** (ADR-0026 re-anchored), which is a Trade's reason to exist beside a Shop: **it quotes the shop's price and costs no slot.** One rate in every zone (`tier × rate` — no depth axis); **a bargain and its exact inverse must quote equal**, else one of the pair is a trap and the other free value (the rule outlives the pair that forced it).
- **Reward** — the primitive that grants **cargo** outright and always completes (#132, #133). One kind, not a union: not items (Offer's job — else `[Fight, Reward]` and `[Fight, Offer]` differ only in who chooses). A plain `int`, baked at generation from the node's **own** stakes and no neighbour's (reading a preceding Fight would leave the Derelict `[Offer, Reward]` undefined; a Fight's own payout reads no neighbour, so this holds). Stowed via `ship_stow_cargo`, overflowing per #157. A **boon** — nothing to decline, the one primitive that stops for no decision; a bare `[Reward]` is a coherent encounter whose interaction is arriving.

### Ghost snapshots and async PvP (ADR-0008, amended by ADR-0018)

- **Ghost_Snapshot** — a captured copy of a ship's current state (stats, layout/fittings, captain) plus voyage progress (steps taken, node **stakes**), used as the opponent-ship representation for a Fight. One struct for hand-authored PvE opponents and, eventually, real players' captured state — no separate opponent-config type.
- **`Ghost_Snapshot.progress.site`** — the node's stakes (`Scaling_Site`): a snapshot carries the site, not any one primitive's reading of it — each reading stays recoverable (`voyage_fight_opponent_hull(site)` and friends). Subsumes the separate `zone` field.
- **EncounterResolved** — the Event emitted when an encounter resolves, carrying a fresh `Ghost_Snapshot` of the ship as it leaves the node.
- **Capture cadence** — **once per encounter, at the end of the node's walk** (ADR-0018, #155/#162): per node, never per stage. A **halt** emits (the cursor jumps to the end, the node resolves); a **sinking** does not — the one encounter that leaves no ghost. The Event's "Resolved" and the Sim's `resolved` flag are one fact.
  _Avoid_: emitting a snapshot from a stage's apply proc — the retired cadence, which left Offer and Shop recording nothing.
- **Hold** (scripted use) — a scripted ship submits Hold every round except when auto-taking Break Off; never Press, Commit or Jettison in this slice. Its Input_Source needs no randomness (ADR-0008).

A `Ghost_Snapshot`'s Hull always resets to max at capture — a ghost is a decoupled copy fought independently by multiple opponents, while the real ship's Hull degrades on its own voyage. Hand-authored PvE opponents may set Hull explicitly as a power knob.

### Build variance: tags, effects, and acquisition (ADR-0012, amending ADR-0004)

- **Tag** — a classifying label a Fitting carries, independent of phase and slot size/visibility. Exists to be *counted* by synergy effects, never to restrict placement.
  _Avoid_: type, class — a tag imposes no fit restriction.
- **Tag family** — one of the five values a Tag is drawn from: **Crew, Weapon, Beast, Artifact, Cargo**. Closed.
- **Context-sensitive effect** — an effect whose magnitude is computed at resolve time from context (the owning ship; for combat, the live round). Plain data — no function pointers — so a Ghost_Snapshot can carry it. Since #404 *every* effect is one: every magnitude is an **expression tree**, and "flat" is the tree that is a single `Const`.
  _Avoid_: scripted effect, effect callback — effects are data, not code.
- **Flat effect** — an effect whose tree is a single `Const`. The common case (most roster items and all three starting fittings). Not a kind since #404 — just the smallest tree.
- **Synergy effect** — an effect scaling with the **count of installed fittings matching a selector** (tag / size / visibility) on the owning ship. A multi-tag fitting counts once per tag. A tree can also count via a `Count` node; the field is shorthand for the whole magnitude scaling by one count.
- **Conditional effect** — an effect whose tree contains a **Gate**, so its yield depends on the round. The old closed set of five triggers is now five authored trees (`item_below_hull_percent`, `item_from_round`, `item_while_concealed`, `item_while_opponent_faster/_slower`), and anything else an author can write is equally sayable (#404).
  _Avoid_: `Condition`, `condition_met` — deleted.
- **Scouting report** — the **only** way the opponent enters an expression: a flattened counter block (`ship_scouting_report`) filtered to what is **Exposed**, carried on the round's facts. Never a ship pointer — no tree reaches a hull, magnitude or slot it wasn't shown, and concealment counters reading **by construction** (a concealed fitting isn't in the census).
- **Two-pass context build** — forced by the layering rule: pass one resolves `Modify_Speed` effects with **no speeds in the context** (`Round_Facts`, `ship_effect_context_pre_speed`); pass two resolves the phases against the speeds pass one produced (`Speeds`, `ship_effect_context_in_battle`). The whole restriction is one line — **a `Modify_Speed` tree reads no speed** — enforced at authoring time (`effect_modify_speed`), never as a runtime zero (a forbidden read resolving to 0 *is* the dead-conditions defect this replaced).
- **Site scale** — a percent riding beside an effect's tree (`Effect.site_scale`), applied to the evaluator's result, rounded half-up once per effect: how a Fight site makes a hostile bite deeper without rewriting an authored number. **Baked at hostile construction and nowhere else** (ADR-0031); moves only `Phase_Contribution` effects (guarded on the verb — Repair and `Modify_Speed` excluded by construction); nothing scales the player's items. Not a node (a wrapping percent would tax the roster a node; scaling constant leaves is wrong — a constant may be a gate's threshold). Honest value **100**, set by the `effect_*` helpers.
- **Showcase reading** — an effect read as an **item card** rather than a round (`expr_showcase`, `effect_showcase_magnitude`): every gate open, every count 1. For offer/refit screens and content tests, where resolving against nothing would print every conditional as "+0". Never called from combat.
- **Stat-modifier effect** — an effect adjusting the owning ship's **effective Speed** — the last stat a fitting can move (Durability left with ADR-0026, Max Hull with ADR-0027). Escape reads the *effective* Speed, never the raw field.
- **Repair effect** — the Brace verb (ADR-0027): restores Hull, capped at Max Hull, ahead of the round's damage. Fires once a round unless its **timing** says otherwise.
- **Timing** — *when* an effect resolves across a battle, a closed union of five: `Always` (the zero value — an unnamed timing fires every round, not never), `Once_Per_Battle`, `Every_N{n}`, `Ramp{per_round, cap}`, `Charge{cost, per_round}`. **The battle is the hard ceiling**: timing state is one counter per (side, slot, effect) on the Battle, zeroed at start — none reaches a Ghost_Snapshot, and out-of-combat timing does not exist. A stat-modifier effect may not carry one (ADR-0029).
- **Splash / Shallow / Deep tier** — the three power/cost grades a roster item is authored at, echoing Coastal → Open Sea → The Deep. A catalog-authoring axis, not a runtime system.
- **Refit** — rearranging fittings by hand through Sim install/move/remove commands, under the fit rule. **No inventory**: a fitting pulled fully off is discarded; every change emits an Event.
  _Avoid_: inventory management — nothing holds an un-installed fitting.
- **Item Offer** — the repurposed Upgrade Offer: a few distinct roster items (or skip), placed via Refit. Free. Now the **Offer** primitive (ADR-0014).
- **Shop** — the primitive presenting purchasable roster items; buying spends **cargo** and places through Refit (a purchase makes the ship lighter and heavier at once; exact spend-under-weight interaction is fogged to the migration). Stock is seed-baked; buying refills the shelf from the stock behind it until you **leave** (completes the stage). Each buy in a visit costs more than the last (**cost escalation with purchase depth**, #124) — the surcharge is what the shelf window earns its keep on (ADR-0015). **Not Port-exclusive** — Shop is the *revealing* stage, so a Port is just `[Shop]` while a merchant at sea hides its Shop behind a leading stage (ADR-0016). Cross-visit persistence is dropped (ADR-0014 superseding ADR-0013; `port_shelves` collapses). The **one primitive that reads no stakes** (ADR-0015): the market is fixed, the gradient is the cargo you bring. Minimal economy: fixed starting budget, Offers free, purchases paid, no sell-back.
- **Stock pool** — the Shop's content roster (ADR-0015, #137): a named pool authoring **which** items (a set of Tag families, or no filter) and **how deep** the hold is. **Named by the recipe** rather than drawn, because a Port and a merchant must not stock alike: the Port's pool is the **Chandlery** — no filter, deep enough that the shelf outlasts the starting cargo — while specialists are one family and shallow enough to buy out. "Differently" means subset and size — deliberately not tier weighting (the stakes question, which Shop reads none of) or price (#124's surcharge is the price knob).

### The expression language (issue #393's spec)

- **Expression tree** — the arithmetic an Effect's magnitude is written in: a bounded, allocation-free, POD language of ten node kinds, evaluated by `expr_eval(tree, ctx)`. A tree is a **value** — inline fixed array, no pool, no pointers — costing a Ghost_Snapshot nothing beyond its bytes. Stored in **prefix order** (arity implies structure; no child indices). **Bound: 12 nodes per effect**, no depth bound (depth can't exceed node count); every arity is even, so well-formed trees have odd node counts and the largest admitted is 11.
  _Avoid_: node pool, node ID; scripted magnitude — the language is data, not code, and has **no RNG**.
- **Quantity** — the closed set a tree may read off the round: own Hull and Max Hull, own and opponent Speed, the round number, the **captain's own order**, damage taken last round, own visibility. The order is **one ordinal** (`Hold=0, Press_Brace=1, Press_Fire=2, Commit=3`), never four flags — four would sell one item at two prices — and only `Eq`/`Ne` may compare it (rejected at authoring otherwise). Jettison and Break Off land as Hold; the **opponent's order is unreadable** (a scripted order is a constant, carrying no information). No `Count(empty slots)` — a vacated slot backfills a hold, so it's the same fact as `count(Tag.Cargo)`.
- **Node kind** — the closed set of ten: `Const, Quantity, Count, Add, Sub, Mul, Min, Max, Pct, Gate`. **No `Div`** — a subtree divisor can be zero and truncation is non-associative; `Pct` pins the divisor at a literal 100. **No `Clamp`/`Sat_Sub`/`Abs`/`Sign`** — each saves a node and costs a permanent calibration row plus a second spelling of one intent.
- **Gate** — the only conditional and only comparison, arity 4 (`op` on the node; `lhs, rhs, then, else` children). **No booleans**: a comparison is consumed where it's made, never a value, so "multiply by" and "select on" a comparison can't be one item at two prices. `and` is nesting; `or` costs a duplicated branch. The sole hook for the pricing model's conditionality discount.
- **Expr_Context** — everything a tree may read, flattened to plain data: a scalar per Quantity, a pre-counted census a Count selects over, and the opponent's (the scouting report). The evaluator takes **no Ship, no layout, no Battle** — testable as arithmetic. Who fills the context is the resolution wiring's business.
- **Authoring helper** — the `expr_const` / `expr_add` / `expr_gate` family, the only way a tree is written. Arity lives in the signature, so a malformed tree is a compile error; the node bound is spent (and asserted) as the tree is built.

### The power budget (issue #393's spec; figures live in `core/ship/budget.odin`)

The tables and rates below are **published in `core/ship/budget.odin`** and applied by `roster_check` — read the figures there; this glossary owns the concepts.

- **Power budget** — the arithmetic deciding whether an authored item is legal: a **size × tier cell grants an allowance**, every effect and every unit of cargo capacity spends against it, and legality is a **band** around the grant. **An allowance, not a derivation** — a derivation would hand every author a free 3× through the three-effect cap. The cost formula is a linear equation an author **rearranges**, not a predicate they test: authoring is *solve, never search* (a checker stops at the first green light; a formula missing a term still returns green).
- **Point** — the budget's unit: **1 point = 1 magnitude of Fire = 5 hull of swing over the reference fight** (Open Sea, baseline 5 rounds, 100 hull). Fire is the numéraire at 1.0 pt/magnitude by construction. Held in hundredths (`Points`).
- **Grant table** — the nine size × tier cells, `size_base + size_rank × tier_index`: *tier buys more in a bigger slot*. Convex in size — which is not a ratio bound; a steeper size ladder would leave the tier ladder decreasing.
- **Band** — legality, **two-sided** (± a fraction of the cell's grant, never narrower than ±1, on the **total** cost): an item too weak for its slot is rejected as loudly as one too strong.
- **Verb rate** — what one magnitude of a verb costs. Repair carries **two rates** (sustained and burst) because press-competitiveness floor and turtle ceiling are opposite demands on one number — the timing axis reconciles them. The burst rate is earned when each firing is paid for: by frequency (`Once_Per_Battle`, `Charge`, `Every_N`) or by cost (a **Commit** gate — never a Press one, which is free).
- **Gate factor** — the conditionality discount, and the only node kind that carries one (the other nine publish 1.0, `Count` included — a tag count resolves at purchase time and the purchase is optional). **A gate the author controls gets no discount**; uncertain gates discount by how uncertain. Factors multiply and floor. **Suppressed for Repair**, where burst already *is* the discount — making gates free distinctness on a burst repair.
- **Weight allowance** — weight is the **one sanctioned way to be slow**, converting into and out of allowance rather than into cost (via the Speed equation and the `Modify_Speed` rate). Per-size defaults with a capped deviation. **Negative magnitudes never refund allowance, for any verb** — a drawback is free exactly when you do not care about it.
- **Capacity cost** — cargo capacity **spends** allowance, and **low bulk is never rewarded**: the grant table already priced full bulk; refunding would double-count. Capacity buys only the *option* on a windfall that would otherwise spill — a carried unit is already a wash against its weight.
- **Count peak** — the most of a countable thing one ship can hold, **derived by walking the real ship template** (`ship_count_peaks`), never transcribed. So **the ship layout is a balance surface**: re-sizing a hold re-prices every counting item. Two distortions recorded, not fixed: opponent counts read the hostile's layout, and the structural tag peak overstates the realistic one.
- **`roster_check`** — **production** code (`ship.roster_check`): pure arithmetic over one authored `Roster_Item`, naming the offender. It runs after the write (the item must compile to exist), so it *confirms* arithmetic the author already did. Every **ripple** invariant reaching past one item stays **test-only in `core/voyage`** (hostile straddle, stock-pool depth vs tag-family headroom, archetype name resolution, trade-name/item-name collision) — `core/ship` cannot import `core/voyage`.
  _Avoid_: a distinctness metric, quota or fingerprint — **distinctness is judgement, permanently**; and no permanent production code may guard the placeholder roster or archetypes.
