# Context: Fantasy Ship Game

Single-context repo. See `docs/adr/` for the decisions behind these terms.

## Glossary

### Engine core (headless/UI boundary)

**Sim**:
The single mutable core simulation state for a voyage — ships, crew, combat, RNG. Has no dependency on rendering or input devices; the same `Sim` runs identically headless or under a UI.
_Avoid_: Game state, world

**Command**:
An operation submitted to the Sim that mutates its state (e.g. submitting a captain's decision). The only way presentation is allowed to affect the Sim.
_Avoid_: Action, input

**Event**:
A fact emitted by the Sim describing something that happened (e.g. `DamageDealt`, `AwaitingCaptainDecision`). The only way presentation learns what happened inside the Sim.
_Avoid_: Message, notification

**Tick**:
One call to the Sim's step procedure. Resolves an entire round of auto-battler logic instantly and batch-emits that round's Events. Not tied to real/wall-clock time — a Tick is a logical sim step, not a rendered frame.
_Avoid_: Frame, step (when referring to rendering)

**run_session**:
The single driver loop, shared by headless and UI, that calls Tick, dispatches Events to an Event_Sink, and asks an Input_Source for a captain's decision whenever the Sim is awaiting one.

**Input_Source**:
The pluggable source `run_session` asks for a captain's decision. Headless mode supplies a scripted or seeded-random one; UI mode supplies one that renders a decision menu and blocks until the player picks.

**Event_Sink**:
The pluggable destination `run_session` dispatches a Tick's Events to. Headless mode logs or records them; UI mode plays them back with animation, blocking until playback finishes.

**Headless mode**:
Running a session with no rendering — used for tests, simulation, and ghost-battle resolution. Compile-time incapable of importing the rendering library (a separate executable, not a runtime flag).
_Avoid_: Simulation mode, test mode

**UI mode**:
Running a session with a human player and real-time rendering.
_Avoid_: Game mode, client mode

### Ship & crew model (weight economy: ADR-0020)

- **Ship** — a voyage's vessel. Owns a small set of top-level stats (Hull, Max Hull, and a Speed *calibration* `base`) and a fixed **layout**. Combat power comes from what's installed in the layout, not from the ship directly — and since ADR-0020 so does the ship's **weight** and the **cargo** it carries: there is no `starting_treasure` field — a ship's cargo *is* its wealth, stowed in the holds — and Speed is **derived** from weight rather than authored.
- **Hull** — the ship's health, persisting across an entire voyage rather than resetting per encounter. Reaching 0 sinks the ship and ends the voyage (permadeath). Restored only by **Repair** — the Brace phase's verb (ADR-0027) and a Trade's Hull gain — and never above **Max Hull**. See ADR-0006.
  _Avoid_: **Durability** — a flat per-hit damage reduction (`final_damage = max(0, raw_damage − Durability)`) that #396 deleted (ADR-0026). One side of that subtraction was site-multiplied and the other was mandated small, so absorption decayed from ~47% at Coastal to ~15% in The Deep and a slot bought ~4x more offence than defence. Damage now lands whole and Hull is the only thing between a hit and a sunk ship.
- **Speed** — the stat governing when a ship may disengage from combat: once a battle passes its baseline round count, the higher-Speed ship may break off. Also breaks ties in a same-round mutual kill and in round-cap stalemate resolution. Since ADR-0020 it is **derived, not authored**: `speed = base + modifiers − weight/10`, still an `int` (every escape gate, tie-break, and opponent-speed comparison in an item's tree needs integer comparison). `base` is one calibration constant — whatever makes the starting ship read `STARTING_SPEED :: 4` — so `Ship.speed` stops varying between ships; a ship's Speed varies with **what it carries**. Weight is a **subtrahend, not a replacement**: `Modify_Speed` items and Speed-granting Trade axes still land in `modifiers`. **Floors at 0** as an authoring invariant, asserted (`base − weight/10 >= 0`), never a live clamp — 0 means the ship is *too laden to run* ("sails in"), reached by getting rich, not a broken number. See ADR-0006 (amended) and ADR-0020.
  _Avoid_: Initiative, turn order — combat resolves simultaneously by fixed phase, not by Speed-based turn order. Authored/base Speed — Speed is read off weight, not stored per ship.
- **Weight** — what a ship's Speed is read against: `weight/10` is subtracted from its Speed, so the divisor is the **exchange rate between cargo and Speed** (10 is forced — any coarser makes jettisoning a Small hold buy 0 Speed). A fitting weighs **its own authored mass plus the cargo stowed in it, 1:1** — `weight + cargo_held`. The two used to be alternatives (a cargo fitting weighed its stack, everything else its authored figure); once any fitting can carry they are terms of one sum, so a bare hold contributes only its cargo, a gun only its mass, and a laden gun both. Guns are permanently heavy and cargo only while stowed: **emptiness, not loadout, is what varies a ship's Speed.** Weight, capacity, and cargo are one scale (a unit of cargo weighs exactly 1), so weight and cargo are **not** `distinct` types — "cargo is weight" is true at the value level (ADR-0011, ADR-0020).
  _Avoid_: mass, tonnage — and do **not** use weight for **bulk**: weight is *mass*, bulk is *volume*, and keeping them apart is what makes a fitting that both carries and fights authorable at all. Note weight is an `int` in the same units as cargo.
- **Bulk** — the **volume a fitting's own machinery takes inside its slot** (`Fitting.bulk`), and the axis every fitting sits on: whatever the slot's size contribution leaves over is what the fitting can carry (`capacity = contribution − bulk`, derived, never stored). An ordinary item authors its full contribution and so carries nothing; a **hold** authors 0 and carries the whole slot. Authored, and **its zero value is the carrying end** — an omitted bulk is a free full-slot hold bolted to a gun — so every authored fitting names it, and a roster item names it by **default**: `roster_item`'s `bulk` parameter defaults to the size's full contribution, so the hazard is closed at the one seam the fifty items are authored through (`the_roster_carries_nothing` guards an entry that overrides it). Bulk is also what a captain spends to buy **jettison granularity**: tonnage smeared across part-full fittings sheds finer than one big hold. (#399; what it costs the power budget is #384's.)
  _Avoid_: bulk as a synonym for weight or for slot size — it is neither the mass nor the container.
- **Starting treasure** — **retired** (ADR-0020), the word along with the field. Was a one-time scalar of starting capital (`Ship.starting_treasure`, `STARTING_TREASURE :: 50`), described here as "not a capacity". Under the weight economy a ship's starting wealth **is** the cargo stowed in its holds — no scalar field survives — and the amount is owned by `STARTING_CARGO :: 40` (ship config) + `CAPTAIN_STARTING_CARGO :: 10` (the captain) = 50, water-filled across the ship's holds by `ship_stow_cargo(layout, amount)`. "A full hold" is now **derived** from that sum, not a constant.
  _Avoid_: starting_treasure, a scalar purse — a ship's wealth is the cargo in its holds.
- **Layout** — the fixed set of **slots** a ship template defines. No grid/coordinates and no shape packing — slots are discrete, sized, named containers.
- **Slot** — a single unit of ship layout. Has a **size** (small / medium / large) and a **base visibility** (exposed / concealed). Slot names (e.g. "gun deck") are flavor only and impose no restriction on what can fill them.
- **Fitting** — the single, unified concept for anything that occupies a slot: crew members, weapons, holds/quarters, or other fantasy entities (creatures, magical objects). Deliberately not split into parallel type systems. A fitting has a size, an effective visibility, a name, a set of **tag families**, **up to three effects** (`effects` plus `effect_count`, the cap in the type — ADR-0029), a **bulk**, and the **cargo it holds**. It has no category of its own: the round phase rides on each effect, so one fitting may feed both phases at once. Since #399 there is **no special-cased cargo kind** — `is_cargo` and `stack_count` are deleted, carrying is an axis every fitting sits on, and the fit rule is an exact size match and nothing else. `cargo_held` is the **one field that moves at runtime**; everything else is authored, which is what lets a selector read a fitting's tags without reading state the power budget cannot see.
- **Tag family** — one of five families (Crew, Weapon, Beast, Artifact, Cargo) a fitting belongs to, independent of its combat phase/category. A fitting carries a *set* of them: usually one, occasionally more (multi-tag is allowed but used sparingly). This is the axis the build-variance effort's synergy effects count fittings along; on its own it drives no behavior. **`Cargo` means "this fitting's job is carrying", not "this fitting is carrying"** — its old referent went with the special-cased cargo kind (#399). It is **authored, never derived**: a gun that happens to be holding cargo does not gain it, because `cargo_held` is the one mutable field and a tag that appeared with it would be a selector reading live state.
- **Cargo** — since ADR-0020 **the ship's wealth**: there is no money aboard — cargo is the one thing you carry, worth something only in trade — and it weighs its contents 1:1 on top of whatever holds it. Since #399 it is **no longer a kind of fitting** but an **axis every fitting sits on**: anything with bulk to spare can hold some, so a gun can also carry and a captain's slot spent on offence is no longer automatically a slot spent against their purse. It contributes nothing to a combat *phase*, but it is what you spend, what a Reward fills, and what a wreck pays out. (ADR-0004, amended by ADR-0020 and #399.)
  _Avoid_: "a cargo fitting" as a *kind*, and "stack" for what one holds — carrying is an axis, not a type; and **money / treasure / purse** — cargo is the one word for what you carry now.
- **Hold** — since #399 a **real installable fitting**: the degenerate corner of the cargo axis — zero bulk, zero mass of its own, no effects, Cargo-tagged — minted by one constructor (`ship_fitting_hold`) at Small / Medium / Large. It is **free**: never a `Roster_Item`, so it has no tier **by construction**, costs no gold and no allowance, and never appears in a shop; the roster stays at exactly 50. Every slot a loadout does not claim carries one, and **removing a fitting backfills one in the vacated slot**, so an empty slot is never something a captain has to manage. "Your hold is full" still reads collectively — cargo has met **cargo capacity**.
  _Avoid_: "cargo filler" and the runtime-minted cargo fitting it named — that is deleted; a hold is installed, not conjured.
- **Cargo capacity** — the ceiling on cargo a ship can carry: summed over its **installed fittings**, each contributing its size contribution **less its bulk** (Small 10 / Medium 20 / Large 40 — ×10 and doubling, a Large hold is four Smalls). The starting 8-slot hull still reads **90**: three guns at full bulk, five holds. **An empty slot carries nothing** — forced, not chosen, because otherwise a free zero-bulk hold would be byte-identical to leaving the slot empty and would exist purely as a farmable Cargo-tagged token. The accepted consequence is that an **empty slot is wasted, not neutral**, which is why the starting ship ships with holds and why a removal backfills one. The ceiling is **structural**: cargo lives inside fittings, which live only in finite slots, so overflow above capacity is **lost** (free re-stowing outside battle is what makes that fair). Derived by `ship_cargo_capacity`, never stored; `Ship.base_cargo_capacity` / `Captain.cargo_capacity_bonus` and their constants are **deleted** (room no slot provided). ADR-0020, #157, #399.
  _Avoid_: a baseline capacity stat, a stored number, and "empty slots count" — capacity comes from what is installed.
- **Stowage** — how a cargo total lands in the fittings that can hold it (`ship_stow_cargo(layout, amount)`): **water-filling**. Equal absolute shares are poured over everything with capacity, each capped at its own room, the unused share cascading to whoever can still take it; the odd units left when no share divides go to the largest remaining room, ties to the lower slot index. It replaced smallest-slot-first, which put fine change in the small slots and so let a *poor* ship heave a full Small for a Speed gain a rich one had to buy with a whole Large — granularity is a property of the **build** (how bulk is authored across the layout) rather than of how little you happen to carry. The result is a **pure function of `(amount, the capacities present)`** — arrangement moves nothing but the tie-break — which is what lets every caller pass a scalar total and re-derive the arrangement from it. #399.
  _Avoid_: smallest-slot-first — retired with the granularity property it encoded.
- **Fit rule** — a fitting may only occupy a slot of the exact matching size (see ADR-0004 for why, and for the flagged extension point).
- **Effective visibility** — a fitting's visibility as actually observed by an opponent (e.g. when scouting a ghost snapshot), resolved through a three-layer precedence: slot base visibility → fitting-level override → ship/captain-level forced override. See ADR-0005.
- **Captain** — a voyage-start choice, structurally separate from the slot system (not a fitting, consumes no slot). Can influence a ship's slot limits/structure and grants additional manual per-round captain actions. Since ADR-0020 the captain also **names part of the starting cargo**: `Captain.starting_cargo_bonus` (`CAPTAIN_STARTING_CARGO :: 10` for Odessa) adds cargo into the ship's headroom — a *fillable* lever, unlike the deleted `cargo_capacity_bonus` (which promised room no slot provides). This keeps `Captain` a live struct rather than a vestigial name.

### Combat resolution (see ADR-0006, amended by ADR-0017, ADR-0025 and ADR-0028)

- **Round** — one unit of battle resolution: the captain's decision is applied, then fitting effects resolve through the Brace → Fire phases. Corresponds to one Tick.
- **Phase** — one of the two fixed stages (Brace, Fire) a round resolves through, in that order. Both ships resolve a phase together (simultaneous, not sequential-by-Speed); a ship's own fittings within a phase trigger in fixed slot order. **Every effect names the phase that consumes it** (ADR-0029), so a fitting feeds as many phases as it has effects for; **a phase has one verb**: Brace repairs, Fire deals damage (`ship_verb_phase`). The `effect_*` authoring helpers set an effect's phase from its verb, so the two cannot be authored into disagreement; a roster test is the guard that no hand-built literal has.
  _Avoid_: **Muster** — a third phase (resolving first, its output folded 1:1 into Fire) that #314 removed (ADR-0025). Post-#151 a Muster fitting differed from a Fire fitting only by which Press doubled it and by a resolve-first ordering nothing consumed, so it did not earn its category; its fittings are now Fire, and "crew vs guns" rides on the Tag families instead. Brace's own "does it earn its category?" doubt is answered by **Repair** (ADR-0027) — not by arithmetic but by an ordering the death check consumes.
- **Brace** — the phase that resolves **repair**: a side's pressed Brace output is Hull restored to its own ship, applied **before any Fire output lands**, so a repair can save a captain on the round they would otherwise have sunk. That ordering is the whole reason Brace is a phase rather than a summing pass, and it is read by exactly one thing, the death check. Repair fills the *gap* to Max Hull, never more — a repair into a full hull is a no-op that emits nothing. Both phases are **totalled before either writes to a hull**, so a Hull-gated fitting reads the hull its captain saw when they gave the order.
  _Avoid_: treating Brace as damage reduction — nothing is subtracted from a hit (ADR-0026); a repair is added on the other side of it. Site-scaled repair — a hostile repair reaching the player's per-round Fire output is an unkillable hostile, the same zero-damage floor ADR-0019 barred bulwark from.
- **Fire** — the phase that resolves the round's damage: a side's pressed Fire output *is* its raw damage. Holds both the gun fittings and the former crew (ex-Muster) fittings since #314 (ADR-0025).
- **Raw damage** — a side's pressed Fire output, and since ADR-0026 the *whole* of the damage that lands: `final_damage = raw`. Nothing is subtracted, so there is no floor to clamp at.
  _Avoid_: **bulwark** (formerly *soak*, ADR-0021) — the subtracted term, the target's effective Durability plus its own pressed Brace output, deleted by #396. Its "deliberately the smaller vocabulary" principle went with it: raw was free to grow with the site while bulwark was barred from it (ADR-0019), which is the asymmetry that made the defensive half unplayable rather than merely thin.
- **Order** — what a captain submits for a round, one per round, from a closed set of exactly five: **Press / Commit / Jettison / Break Off / Hold** (ADR-0028). Every one is plain data, and the set costs a ghost snapshot no new state — the only battle-scoped fact any of them adds is Press's spent flag. Which of them an item may **read** is a narrower set than the five: Jettison is excluded (#400), because a once-a-voyage panic is not a choice to reward an item for; nothing reads any of them yet (`Battle_State` carries the round and the two speeds and no order at all).
  _Avoid_: **Man the Sails** (a temporary per-round Speed increase) and **Reallocate** (pouring cargo between two of one's own fittings) — both deleted by #398. Man the Sails granted Speed on top of a stat ADR-0020 had already made emergent from weight, and its bonus reset before the escape check that was its only consumer; Reallocate bought jettison granularity, which #399 made a property of how a build authors bulk, and had had no UI since #305.
- **Press** — the captain's order multiplying **its own phase's fittings** by `PRESS_MULTIPLIER :: 3`, this round only (ADR-0006's "that phase's fitting output", restored by ADR-0017), and available **at most once per battle** (ADR-0028). It targets Brace or Fire, and **both arms read**: Press Fire triples the damage dealt, Press Brace the Hull restored. The ration is the whole point — free and unlimited, the only question it asks is *whether*; rationed, it asks *when* — and the multiplier is 3 because a smaller bump does not repay the timing.
  _Avoid_: a per-round Press, or a Press that recharges — one per fight, tracked by a flag on the Battle (`combat_may_press`), and a second one submitted in the same fight is a driver bug the combat layer asserts on.
- **Commit** — Press's every-round sibling that pays for itself: **x2 Brace, Fire to zero**, at `COMMIT_MULTIPLIER :: 2` (ADR-0028). **One-directional by construction** — the mirrored form (x2 Fire, Brace to zero) would strictly dominate it — and because a committing captain deals no damage that round, Commit can never *win* a fight, only survive one. Press is Commit without the sacrifice, and that is precisely what is rationed.
  _Avoid_: a symmetric Commit, and a rationed one — the sacrifice is what pays for it being available every round.
- **Break Off** — the captain's order, available once a ship's Speed exceeds its opponent's after the battle's baseline round count, that ends the battle immediately for both ships without destruction. ADR-0006's "primary tool for avoiding a voyage-ending mistake"; the escape it opens is read off Speed alone, which nothing in a battle grants any more (ADR-0028 deleted the one order that did).
- **Hold** — the stance of taking no other order: behaviourally a no-op, but a **named, player-selectable order** on the fight menu rather than the absence of a decision, and a fact an item can gate on (ADR-0028). It stays a named `Command` variant rather than a nil command, because nil encodes "the driver submitted nothing", a different fact. A scripted (non-player-controlled) ship submits it every round it isn't automatically taking Break Off.
  _Avoid_: nil as "the captain held", and "Hold" for a lull in the fight — it is an order given.
- **Jettison Cargo** — a captain decision that **empties one fitting's cargo, re-stows the remainder, and emits the event** — nothing more. Since #399 the **fitting stays in its slot**: what goes over the side is the cargo, not the thing holding it, so a laden gun survives having its load heaved and an emptied hold is still the ship's capacity. (Under the old model a cargo fitting *was* its cargo, so emptying it and nulling the slot were one act.) Since ADR-0020 the Speed gain is not a bonus but the **weight drop itself**: a lighter ship is faster for free (`speed = base + modifiers − weight/10`), so `JETTISON_SPEED_BONUS` and the per-battle `battle.perm_speed` retire. Jettisoned cargo is **always destroyed** — never claimed as spoils (the claim was never observable and would make jettison free on a win), so `combat_settle_jettisoned_cargo`, `battle.jettisoned`, and issue #52's run-scoped arena block are deleted. A fitting carrying nothing weighs nothing extra, so it cannot be jettisoned for free Speed (`combat_apply_jettison` asserts `cargo_held > 0`). How coarse a jettison is falls out of the **build** rather than of how rich you are: water-filling spreads cargo evenly, so what you can heave in one go is set by how bulk was authored across the layout. Since #400 the heave **re-stows what is left** (`ship_stow_cargo` over the whole layout, the same water-fill every out-of-battle cargo change lands in), which makes jettison **self-flattening**: the slot heaved twice holds a smaller share the second time, so every heave sheds no more than the last and the escape window closes as it is used rather than staying open at a flat price per round. It is **one order that names its target in a second step** — the fight menu's row becomes the laden fittings and clicking one heaves it — so the order set stays at five however many holds the ship has, and that second step **is** the confirmation: an in-battle jettison is never confirmed again. It is also **excluded from the readable order set**: a once-a-voyage panic priced as a bomb is not something an item's expression trees may be authored to reward. It is **one act on two surfaces, priced differently** (#401): under fire it is an order and costs the round; **at anchor it is free and repeatable**, so a captain can lighten deliberately before sailing into hidden water. Both go through one primitive (`ship_jettison_cargo`), so the emptying, the fitting staying put and the re-stow are defined once. The at-anchor burn is a **drag onto the hold ledger** and is **confirmed** — encounters are hidden and cargo *is* the score, so a misdrag costs the run; the discard bin beside it keeps its own meaning, *this thing leaves the ship*, and is never the burn target (a laden gun must not become unremovable). Accepted consequence: weight is a **free precision dial at anchor**, so the granularity a build authors binds only in battle, where the round cost binds. See ADR-0006 (amended), ADR-0020, ADR-0028.
  _Avoid_: a permanent Speed bonus, jettisoned-cargo spoils — the Speed is the weight, and the cargo is gone. Also avoid **"Jettison" for putting a *fitting* off the ship**: the word names the cargo act, and the Build surface's discard bin reads *Over the Side*.
- **Mast configuration** — the **cosmetic read-out** of a ship's Speed, never an input: the masts *show* what the ship's weight already decided, so it is compatible with ADR-0020's single-constant `base`. Speed **0** renders as **"sails in"** — a ship too laden to run — rather than as a number that looks broken; because 0 is reached by *getting rich*, this is a mainline reading, not an edge case. New vocabulary (ADR-0020, #175): the game has *Storm Sails* / *Spare Rigging* but no prior mast concept.
  _Avoid_: mast as a Speed input or a stat — it is a display of derived Speed.

### Above a voyage (see issue #278; loop architecture in ADR-0022)

- **Chart Table** — the one screen above a voyage, and the whole of the game outside one: where a captain stands over a chart and decides to sail. The exe **boots** into it, and every voyage is launched from it. It holds a title, **Begin a voyage**, and **Quit** — nothing else — over the chart itself (the menu background; see the effort's carve-out). There is exactly one screen up here: the "start menu" and the return screen are the **same** screen, because the Chart Table is **stateless** — it holds no voyage outcome and looks identical at boot and after a sinking. That is what lets a screenshot taken at frame 0 be an honest photograph of the real thing.

  "Chart" deliberately means what it already means — the voyage's node graph (ADR-0009) — and the overlap is the point rather than a collision: at the Chart Table there is **no chart yet**, and beginning a voyage is what draws one. ADR-0021 rejected *Chart* as a name for `Recipe` because it "collides with the map"; a recipe is not a map, but the Chart Table **is** about the map, so the same overlap that disqualified it there qualifies it here. `Start`, `Port`, and `Haven` were unavailable — all three are map nodes *inside* a voyage — which is why the obvious harbour words are not used.

  Meta-progression would attach **here** if the game ever wants it. It does not today: voyages accrete nothing, and the Chart Table shows no record of past ones. This is a **signpost, not a promise** — whether the game has meta-progression at all is an open design question this term does not settle (see Voyage).
  _Avoid_: start menu, main menu, shell, title screen — the words the request arrived in, none of which are this game's. A stateful Chart Table, or a second screen above a voyage.

- **The end-of-voyage beat belongs to the voyage, not the Chart Table.** Both endings — Haven reached and permadeath — are told by the voyage, which owns the state that makes them mean anything (hull, cargo, which node you sank at). It says its piece and hands back to an unchanged Chart Table. The Chart Table never greets you with an outcome; it has none to greet you with.

### Voyage UI spine (in-voyage presentation — see ADR-0024)

The in-voyage UI is **two top-level modes** — Home and Encounter — and nothing else. It sits above `run_session` where ADR-0022 put the Chart Table; these terms describe what the UI does *within* one voyage.

- **Home** — the mode at anchor, **between encounters**, while the Sim awaits the next travel choice. The **Build surface** is its persistent editable ground and the **chart** is a raisable overlay over it. It is the one place the player is free: refit freely, and sail. Reached from the Chart Table at Start and returned to after every resolved encounter; a landmark or already-walked node drops you straight back here.
  _Avoid_: the refit screen, the travel screen — Home is one mode holding both a Build surface and a raisable chart, not either screen alone.
- **Encounter** (mode) — the mode walking a node's stage list: a focused, one-stage-at-a-time takeover, entered by sailing into a node and left when the walk completes or halts. The current stage owns the screen; the chart is raisable here only as a **view-only** reference (see the flick).
- **Build surface** (the Build screen) — the **one** refit surface, and the whole of refit. Persistent at Home, and reused by the Offer and Shop stages with a **shelf** added beside the ship's slots. Available everywhere except inside a **Fight** — the single place refit is locked. The modal `refit_menu_loop` collapses into it. Every edit commits live (each install/move/remove is a command that emits an Event), and there is no inventory (a fitting pulled off is discarded — see **Refit**).
  _Avoid_: a modal refit opened per Offer/Shop; a separate placement UI — placement *is* refit, on this one surface.
- **The flick** (the swipe) — the motion that raises and lowers the chart overlay over the Build surface: **swipe the chart over, swipe it back**, anchored by a corner tab or screen edge. A **mouse drag** (press–drag–release), the one input primitive beyond the game's click-polling; a click on the tab is an acceptable first-build stand-in. The chart is swipe-raisable everywhere, but **sailable only at Home** — mid-encounter its nodes are greyed and unclickable, since travel is not a legal move until the walk ends.
  _Avoid_: arrow keys or a screen-switch — the flick lays a second live surface over the first, it does not swap screens.
- **The shelf** — the temporary source container an **Offer** or **Shop** stage adds beside the ship's slots on the Build surface: the items on offer / the stock. Taking one is an ordinary refit move (shelf item → slot); the only move that can carry a cost is a **Shop** shelf→ship transfer, which spends cargo (an Offer's is free). Exists only for the duration of the stage.
  _Avoid_: an inventory — the shelf is a stage-scoped source, not a store the player carries between nodes.
- **Playback layer** — the single shared, blocking beat overlay any stage invokes on top of itself; every `Event_Sink` beat renders through it (a Fight's per-round Events, arrival, a Reward's grant, a halt, a wreck's loot, the voyage-end beat). It is a shared *surface* for the `Event_Sink`'s output, not a change to its blocking contract (ADR-0002, ADR-0022).
  _Avoid_: each stage drawing its own beats — playback is one layer, not five.

### Map & voyage structure (see ADR-0009, superseding ADR-0007's topology/ports/gradient; encounter terms amended by ADR-0014)

- **Voyage** — one playable attempt from Start to Haven (or to permadeath), launched from the **Chart Table** and returning to it at either ending. Not resumable/saved — save/resume and meta-progression between voyages are not yet specified; #278 named the Chart Table as where meta-progression would attach without ruling on whether it should exist.
- **Node** — a single location in the voyage's map. The map is a procedurally-generated connected graph, regenerated fresh each voyage (seeded, reproducible); a node carries **edges** to other nodes and, if it's an encounter, a stage list whose content is hidden until arrival unless its **first** stage is a revealing one.
  _Avoid_: point, tile.
- **Edge** — a directed connection between two nodes. Edges are only ever generated forward (layer *i* → *i+1*), but at runtime are walkable both ways: forward into new territory, or backward to an already-visited node.
- **Layer** — nodes are generated in ordered layers (a few wide); edges connect adjacent layers. A node's position across layers within its zone is its **depth**.
- **Zone** — one of three fixed **stakes** bands, generated as three sequential phases in a fixed order: Coastal (nearest Start, lowest stakes) → Open Sea (mid) → The Deep (nearest Haven, highest). Stakes rise with zone. A zone also **hard-maps to an encounter's stage count** — Coastal 1, Open Sea 2, The Deep 3 — so encounters get longer as well as steeper (ADR-0014).
- **Depth** — a node's layer index within its zone's phase, normalized to a fixed range. Stakes rise with depth, stacking on top of the per-zone gradient, for the primitives that read it. Replaces ADR-0007's retired port-proximity ("contested waters") effect.
- **Stakes** — how much is on the line at a node: the single gradient `Scaling_Site` (zone tier + depth) expresses, which each stage primitive reads through its own constants — Fight as opponent power, Reward as cargo, Offer as item quality, Trade as swing size. **A primitive reads as much of the gradient as it has room for, and three read less than all of it.** **Fight reads it as a *factor*, and is the only primitive that multiplies** (ADR-0019): the others read a quantity the site *grants*, so zero is a coherent floor and adding is the whole of what they do, but a hostile arrives already authored and what the site decides is how much of it lands — which an addition cannot say, since it has no way *down*. Fight, Offer and Reward read both axes. **Trade reads the zone tier only**: its constants are an exchange rate rather than a magnitude, and every row quotes in reference-fight Hull swing (ADR-0026 re-anchored the table when Durability, its old smallest row, was deleted), with Max Hull the binding row — a depth step fine enough to read would compound across a zone faster than the tier ladder it refines (#146). **Shop reads it as nothing**: the gradient it faces is the cargo the captain arrives with rather than anything about the node (ADR-0015). Renames ADR-0009's "difficulty" gradient, which was one axis named twice and meant nothing for a Trade or an Offer.
  _Avoid_: difficulty — it only ever described the Fight reading.
- **Start** — the voyage's origin node; also the home port. A landmark.
- **Haven** — the voyage's destination node. Reaching it with Hull > 0 wins the voyage. Carries no port and no encounter. A landmark.
- **Landmark** — Start or Haven: a node carrying no encounter (never triggers one) and always fully visible on the map. Port left this category in ADR-0014 — it is an encounter (`[Shop]`) that is visible because it opens on a Shop, not because it is exempt.
- **Port** — an encounter node holding the one-stage `[Shop]` recipe, placed by the Port bucket — two per zone (six) plus Start — each **consuming** one of its zone's node slots rather than being added on top. Visible on the map because it **opens** on a Shop, the revealing stage, not because it is a landmark: a Port is an ordinary encounter and, since ADR-0014, resolves once like any other. It is a Port by two things and no third: **where** it is put (bespoke placement) and **what it sells** (the Chandlery stock pool — ADR-0015). Since ADR-0016 those two collapse into its visibility rather than sitting beside it: only the Port bucket opens on a Shop, so **revealing ⟺ opening on a Shop ⟺ being a Port**, and the six Ports are the only encounters on the map a captain can see coming. There is no `Node_Kind.Port`: nothing about the node says it is one, so "is this a port" is asked of its stage list.
- **Encounter** — a non-landmark node's content: an ordered list of **stages** plus a cursor, walked once on **first** arrival (no decline option); revisiting a node never re-triggers it. The only way to avoid an encounter is to route around its node. There is exactly one Encounter type — the interaction it presents is its stage list, not a kind tag (ADR-0014).
- **Stage** — one step of an encounter: a **stage primitive** plus the content it was baked with. An encounter walks its stages in order, and each resolves to **completed** or **halted**.
- **Stage primitive** — one of five values a stage is drawn from: **Fight, Offer, Trade, Shop, Reward**. A closed set — adding an encounter must never require adding a primitive. A primitive *is* the trait: there is no orthogonal trait layer, and variance comes from each stage's own content roster.
  _Avoid_: encounter kind, trait.
- **Complete / Halt** — the two outcomes of a stage, and the whole of an encounter's control flow. **Completed** advances the cursor to the next stage; **halted** ends the encounter there, keeping whatever earlier stages already granted. The primitive defines which is which — Fight: victory completes, Break Off halts, sinking ends the voyage; Offer: pick completes, skip halts; Trade: accept completes, reject halts; Shop: leaving completes; Reward: always completes. Recipes author **no** gates.
- **Recipe** — a named, authored stage set: the unit of encounter content. `Sea Battle = [Fight, Reward]`; `Derelict = [Offer, Reward]`. Generation **picks a whole recipe** and each of its stages rolls content from its own roster — generation never composes a stage list. Mix-and-match is a developer authoring tool, not a runtime generative system. A recipe authors a **stage spec** — a primitive plus what that primitive cannot draw for itself (ADR-0015) — rather than a bare primitive; today the only such thing is a Shop's **stock pool**, and the field is absent on every other primitive.

  The **catalog** (#138) is the fifteen recipes below and nothing else — the encounters in the game, said aloud. A recipe's name is the noun a stage tuple cannot be, and one **shape** (kind sequence) may be authored once only: all variance below the stage list is drawn per node, so a duplicate shape would be one encounter twice.

  | Bucket | Recipes |
  | --- | --- |
  | 1 stage — Coastal | **Skirmish** `[Fight]` · **Flotsam** `[Offer]` · **Bargain** `[Trade]` · **Drifting Salvage** `[Reward]` |
  | 2 stages — Open Sea | **Sea Battle** `[Fight, Reward]` · **Derelict** `[Offer, Reward]` · **Boarding Action** `[Fight, Offer]` · **Press Gang** `[Fight, Shop]` · **Smuggler's Cove** `[Trade, Shop]` · **Privateer's Toll** `[Trade, Reward]` |
  | 3 stages — The Deep | **Contested Anchorage** `[Fight, Shop, Reward]` · **Sunken Reliquary** `[Offer, Shop, Reward]` · **Prize Convoy** `[Fight, Reward, Shop]` · **Smuggler's Run** `[Trade, Shop, Offer]` · **Kraken's Wake** `[Fight, Trade, Reward]` |
  | Port (bespoke) | **Port** `[Shop: Chandlery]` |

  Two conventions govern authoring, both checked by test rather than by the type system. **Costs precede boons**: a halt is an *exit*, so the two declinable costs (Fight, Trade) are authored ahead of the boons they pay for — `[Offer, Fight]` would let a captain skip an item they never had and dodge the fight for nothing. **Only the Port bucket opens on a Shop**: the map labels a revealed encounter by its first stage, so a `[Shop]` merchant would draw a Port's marker and make "go and restock" a gamble between a Chandlery and a six-card specialist — the exact promise the Port bucket's guaranteed placement exists to keep. A merchant vessel therefore earns its Shop by putting a stage in front of it, which is why **Coastal has no shops but its two Ports**.

  _Avoid_: naming a recipe after its stage tuple; a second recipe with an existing shape.
- **Bucket** — a pool of recipes plus a placement rule. The 1-/2-/3-stage buckets are dealt per zone via a shuffled **recipe bag** (ADR-0009's kind bag, renamed), and membership is **derived** from `len(recipe.stages)`, never authored. The Port bucket (`[Shop]`) is placed two per zone; Start and Haven are fixed and terminal.
- **Encounter kind** — **retired** (ADR-0014). Was: the one type of interaction a node presented (Ship Battle / Upgrade Offer / Stat Trade), as an enum mirrored by a union variant, Sim phases, Commands, Events, and a `sim_*.odin` file per kind. The three kinds are now one-stage recipes: Ship Battle → `[Fight]`, Item Offer → `[Offer]`, Stat Trade → `[Trade]`.
- **Fight** — the stage primitive for a full battle against an opponent ship, resolved via the phased-round combat system (ADR-0006). Mechanically identical to a future ghost-PvP battle; this slice's opponents are not real players' stored snapshots. Draws its opponent from a **hostile roster** (#135), retiring the single hand-tuned opponent template. Since ADR-0020 a Fight also **pays**: **sinking** a ship gives the winner that wreck's hold as it stands (**only a wreck pays** — `voyage_finish_ship_battle` must tell `End_Reason.Destroyed` from `.Round_Cap`; a stalemate pays nothing). At the flat 50% hostile fill (#176) the payout is **30–65** against a Reward's 20–45, so #157's overflow becomes the **mainline** result of winning a Fight — cargo first visibly falls overboard here.
- **Hostile archetype** — one authored entry in the Fight primitive's roster: a named build and the roster items it carries. Since ADR-0020 it **no longer authors a Speed** — `Hostile_Archetype.speed: int` is deleted, and a hostile's Speed is derived from its weight like every other ship's, off a cargo hold filled to a **flat 50% of capacity** (a placeholder until hostile ship *templates* exist — #176, out of scope here). It holds **no Hull or magnitudes** — an archetype is *character*, the node's stakes are *power*, and the two are independent axes, so a Deep node deals a **tougher** hostile rather than a different pool of them. Built from the same ~50 items the player can be offered (ADR-0012), named rather than restated, so hostile and player content cannot drift apart. An entry is authored at **Open Sea weight** — the zone where the site's power factor reads 100% (ADR-0019) — so it describes the hostile a captain meets in the middle zone, half of it in the Coastal shallows, and half again on top in The Deep. Item **order is authoring**: placement is first-empty-fit into the template's exposed slots first, so a later item falls into the concealed hold, which is what decides whether a concealment effect fires.
  _Avoid_: treating an archetype as a difficulty rung ("the Deep one"). Depth is the site's job; an archetype is drawn with no regard to zone.
  _Avoid_: authoring an entry to be met undiminished by a starting ship. That was the pre-ADR-0019 rule, and it is what left the table at Coastal weight — an entry written that low was halved into a starting ship's bulwark and could not scratch it at all. Since ADR-0026 deleted bulwark the failure mode is milder — a halved entry deals half, not nothing — but the authoring rule stands.
- **Hostile Speed** — the one Fight stat the stakes gradient **disowns**: the site never moves it. It replaces a flat constant that pinned every hostile at 5 against a starting ship's 4 — which quietly meant *every* hostile was escape-eligible at the baseline round (so every fight had an escape hatch) while the player, slower than everything afloat, could never take Break Off at all and no "vs a slower foe" item could ever fire. A roster spanning both sides of the player's Speed is what makes all three live. Since ADR-0020 it is no longer an authored field either: a hostile's Speed is **derived from its weight** (a flat 50% hold fill — #176), and because that fill is a *constant*, depth still moves no hostile's Speed — so the site-disowns-it property is **re-derived, not overturned**. But the player's own Speed is now read off their cargo (9…0 across a hold of 0–90), so **straddle is a joint property of (roster, player cargo)**: #135's straddle test comes forward pinned to the starting cargo explicitly (`STARTING_CARGO + CAPTAIN_STARTING_CARGO`), asserting a *point* — ≥1 hostile slower, ≥1 faster — not a window, because leaving the window as you get rich is the feature (#177).
- **Offer** — the stage primitive presenting a few *distinct roster items* (or a skip) and opening a Refit to place or swap the pick. Free. Formerly the **Upgrade Offer** encounter kind (same-category auto-replace of a starting fitting), repurposed as the **Item Offer** by the build-variance effort (ADR-0012, #96) and now a primitive in its own right.
- **Trade** — the stage primitive for a permanent **stat-for-stat** trade-off: one stat gained, one stat spent, both scaled by the node's zone. Draws its axis from a roster (#136); the +Durability / −Speed swap that was the whole of the old Stat Trade kind is gone entirely, both of its stats having left the vocabulary. Tradeable stats are a closed set — **Hull, Max Hull, Cargo** (Speed left with #180, Durability with ADR-0026, which also deleted the Scrapped Armour axis). A Trade is the one thing left that moves Max Hull, and since ADR-0027 buying that ceiling is worth doing: in-battle repair is what fills it. The "or stat-for-cargo capacity" this entry used to promise is **dropped**: cargo *capacity* is a number nothing in the game reads, so trading it would be a no-op on both sides. **ADR-0020 landed the change (#143) that gives a Cargo trade meaning**: cargo is now weight stowed in the holds, so a Cargo axis moves the ship's Speed as a side effect (cargo *is* weight) and can **overflow** if the gain exceeds capacity (gain above capacity is lost, no special case — #157). **Open (#180):** whether a Speed-*cost* axis (Braced Bulkheads) subtracts a modifier or adds ballast, and what bounds a cumulative Trade cost once the hold fills — ADR-0020's never-negative invariant is scoped to the weight term precisely because it cannot cover the modifier term.
- **Trade swing** — the amount of a given stat one "unit" of trade is worth in a zone, and thereby the **exchange rate** between stats: an axis names two stats and each side's magnitude is that stat's swing there, so every trade is one swing for one swing and the whole roster is priced from one table. **A swing was originally one of the zone's stat fittings** (#146): the item roster prices each stat in cargo by tier and a Shop is where a captain converts one into the other, so the swing table read that price list off. ADR-0026 deleted the two stats that anchoring named (Durability, and Speed before it at #180), so the surviving rows now quote in **reference-fight Hull swing** — what a swing is worth in a fight at the same site. That is what gives a Trade its reason to exist beside a Shop: **it quotes the shop's price and costs no slot.** Consequences worth knowing: the rate is the *same in every zone* (one rate, not twelve — a stat's swing is `tier × rate`, which is why there is no depth axis); **a bargain and its exact inverse must be quoted equal**, because any gap between two rows would make one of a pair a permanent trap and the other free value. (The pair that forced the rule, Braced Bulkheads / Stripped Spars, is gone with Durability and Speed; the rule outlives it.) A trade is **all-or-nothing** — a cost the ship can't pay in full (measured against the *effective* stat, floored per stat) can't be accepted, rather than being clamped to what it can afford.
- **Reward** — the stage primitive that grants **cargo** outright and always completes (#132, #133). One kind, not a union: not items (that is Offer's job — a Reward that granted one would make `[Fight, Reward]` and `[Fight, Offer]` differ only in whether the captain got to choose), and not cargo-as-a-distinct-thing (the payout *is* cargo already — ADR-0020 made a ship's wealth literal cargo slots, and a Reward stows it into them via `ship_stow_cargo`, overflowing above capacity per #157). The amount is a plain `int`, baked at generation from the node's **own** stakes and no neighbour's: reading a preceding Fight's opponent would leave `[Offer, Reward]` — the Derelict — undefined, so the opponent's "Spoils" cargo stays flavour. (A *Fight* does now pay out of its own opponent — see Fight — but that reads no neighbour, so `[Offer, Reward]` is untouched.) A Reward is a **boon**: it has nothing to decline, so it is the one primitive that stops for no decision, and a bare `[Reward]` (drifting salvage) is a coherent encounter whose interaction is arriving.

### Ghost snapshots and async PvP (see ADR-0008, amended by ADR-0018)

- **Ghost_Snapshot** — a captured copy of a ship's current state (stats, layout/fittings, captain) plus its voyage progress (steps taken, and the node's **stakes**), used as the opponent-ship representation for a Fight stage. The same struct serves both this slice's hand-authored PvE opponents and, eventually, real players' captured state — there is no separate opponent-config type.
- **`Ghost_Snapshot.progress.site`** — the node's stakes (`Scaling_Site`: zone tier + depth), and what a snapshot records of *where* it was captured. It carries the site rather than any one primitive's reading of it: each primitive's reading stays recoverable from the site (`voyage_fight_opponent_hull(site)` and friends), and no primitive has to claim a number it doesn't own. Subsumes the separate `zone` field, which the site already carries.
- **`Ghost_Snapshot.difficulty_rating`** — **retired as a misnomer** (ADR-0014, #130). It was meant to carry a node's tuned difficulty number, but a Stat Trade has no difficulty — only **stakes** — so `run_apply_stat_trade` shoved `gain_durability` into it, and the code that filled it was lying about what it meant. Replaced by `progress.site`.
  _Avoid_: difficulty_rating, and any per-primitive magnitude standing in for the gradient.
- **EncounterResolved** — the Event the Sim emits when an encounter resolves, carrying a fresh `Ghost_Snapshot` of the ship the captain leaves the node with.
- **Capture cadence** — **once per encounter**, at the end of the node's walk (ADR-0018 amending ADR-0008, #155/#162): per *node*, never per stage, so a `[Fight, Reward]` emits one snapshot taken post-loot and an Offer or Shop emits one carrying what went aboard. A **halt** emits (the cursor jumps to the end and the node resolves); a **sinking** does not (the walk stops dead, the node never resolves) — the one encounter in a voyage that leaves no ghost. The Event's "Resolved" and the Sim's `resolved` flag are one fact.
  _Avoid_: emitting a snapshot from a stage's apply proc — the retired cadence, which was the three procs that happened to return one rather than a decision, and which left Offer and Shop (the stages that change the *build*) recording nothing.
- **Hold** (the scripted ship's use of it) — a scripted (non-player-controlled) ship submits **Hold** every round except when automatically taking Break Off once escape-eligible; it never chooses Press, Commit or Jettison Cargo in this slice. See **Hold** under Combat resolution for the order itself.
  _Avoid_: assuming a scripted opponent's Input_Source needs randomness — it doesn't; see ADR-0008.

A `Ghost_Snapshot`'s Hull always resets to the ship's max/base Hull at capture time, regardless of the real ship's current voyage-persistent Hull — a ghost is a decoupled copy that may be fought independently by multiple opponents, while the real player's own Hull keeps degrading normally on their own voyage. Hand-authored PvE opponents may still set Hull to any explicit value as an opponent-power knob.

### Build variance: tags, effects, and acquisition (see ADR-0012, amending ADR-0004)

- **Tag** — a classifying label a Fitting carries, independent of its combat phase (ADR-0006) and of its slot size/visibility. Exists to be *counted* by synergy effects, not to restrict placement. A fitting may hold more than one; multi-tag is allowed but used sparingly.
  _Avoid_: type, class — a tag imposes no fit restriction, unlike slot size.
- **Tag family** — one of the five values a Tag is drawn from: **Crew, Weapon, Beast, Artifact, Cargo**. A closed set.
- **Context-sensitive effect** — a fitting effect whose magnitude is *computed at resolve time* from the context it sees (the owning ship, and for combat the live round) rather than stored as a fixed number. Plain data — no function pointers — so a Ghost_Snapshot can carry it (ADR-0008). Since #404 every effect is one, because every magnitude is an **expression tree**: "flat" is the tree that happens to be a single `Const`, and what used to be a closed set of five conditions is arithmetic an author writes.
  _Avoid_: scripted effect, effect callback — effects are data, not code.
- **Flat effect** — an effect whose tree is a single `Const`, so it ignores context. The common case: most roster items are flat, and so are the three starting fittings (see ADR-0012). Not a *kind* of effect since #404 — just the smallest tree.
- **Synergy effect** — an effect whose magnitude scales with the **count of installed fittings matching a selector** (over tag / size / visibility), resolved against the owning ship's current layout. Rises as matching fittings are added and falls as they are removed; a multi-tag fitting counts once per tag. A tree can also count for itself with a `Count` node; the field is the shorthand for the whole magnitude scaling by one count.
- **Conditional effect** — an effect whose tree contains a **Gate**, so what it yields depends on the round it is resolved in. Until #404 this was a closed set of five triggers (Hull threshold, round number, own concealment, opponent faster/slower) stored beside the magnitude; those five are now five authored trees (`item_below_hull_percent`, `item_from_round`, `item_while_concealed`, `item_while_opponent_faster/_slower`), and anything else an author can write is equally sayable.
  _Avoid_: `Condition`, `condition_met` — the union and its evaluator are deleted.
- **Scouting report** — the **only** way the opponent enters an expression: a flattened counter block (`ship_scouting_report`), filtered to what is **Exposed**, carried on the round's facts. Never a ship pointer, so no tree can reach a hull, a magnitude or a slot it was not shown — and concealment counters being read **by construction**, since a concealed fitting is simply not in the census.
- **Two-pass context build** — how a round's context is assembled, and **forced by the layering rule rather than chosen**: pass one resolves `Modify_Speed` effects against the round's facts with **no speeds in the context at all** (`Round_Facts`, `ship_effect_context_pre_speed`), and pass two resolves the phases against the speeds pass one produced (`Speeds`, `ship_effect_context_in_battle`). A tree may read any quantity computed strictly below its own layer; Speed is the only stat with a modifier layer, so the whole restriction is one line — **a `Modify_Speed` tree reads no speed, own or opponent** — enforced at authoring time (`effect_modify_speed`), never as a runtime zero. Resolving a forbidden read to 0 *is* the dead-conditions defect this replaced.
- **Site scale** — a percent riding **beside** an effect's tree (`Effect.site_scale`), applied to what the evaluator returns and rounded half-up: how a Fight site makes a hostile's damage bite deeper without rewriting an authored number. Not a node — wrapping the root in a percent would tax the whole roster a node, and scaling constant leaves would be wrong, because a constant may be a gate's threshold. Its honest value is **100**, set by the `effect_*` authoring helpers, since a zero would silently disarm the item.
- **Showcase reading** — an effect read as an **item card** rather than as a round (`expr_showcase`, `effect_showcase_magnitude`): every gate open, every count 1. What an item in the hand is worth, for the offer and refit screens and the content tests, where there is no ship, round or opponent — and where resolving against nothing would print every conditional item as "+0". Never called from combat.
- **Stat-modifier effect** — an effect that adjusts the owning ship's **effective Speed** rather than feeding a combat phase. Speed is the last stat a fitting can move: Durability left the set with ADR-0026 and Max Hull with ADR-0027, whose repair verb fills the ceiling instead of raising it. Escape reads the *effective* Speed (base field plus installed modifiers, less weight), never the raw field; Hull and Max Hull are their own fields, read directly.
- **Repair effect** — the Brace phase's verb (ADR-0027): its resolved magnitude restores the owning ship's Hull, capped at Max Hull, ahead of the round's damage. Triggers once a round, like the Fire effects it mirrors, unless its **timing** says otherwise.
- **Timing** — *when* an effect resolves across a battle, as a closed union of five: `Always`, `Once_Per_Battle`, `Every_N{n}`, `Ramp{per_round, cap}` (fires every round, growing by `per_round` up to `cap`), `Charge{cost, per_round}` (banks its gain and spends it on the round it fires). `Always` is the zero value, so an effect that names no timing fires every round rather than never. **The battle is the hard ceiling**: what a timing remembers is one counter per (side, slot, effect) held on the Battle and zeroed at battle start, so no timing state reaches a Ghost_Snapshot and out-of-combat timing does not exist to be authored. A stat-modifier effect may not carry one at all — its consumer is read outside a battle (ADR-0029).
- **Splash / Shallow / Deep tier** — the three power/cost grades a roster item is authored at — Splash (lightest / cheapest) → Shallow (mid) → Deep (strongest) — echoing the Coastal → Open Sea → The Deep voyage progression. A catalog-authoring axis, not a runtime system.
- **Refit** — rearranging a ship's fittings by hand through Sim install / move / remove commands, enforcing ADR-0004's exact-size fit rule. There is **no inventory**: a fitting pulled fully off the ship is discarded, and every loadout change emits an Event.
  _Avoid_: inventory management — nothing holds an un-installed fitting.
- **Item Offer** — the Upgrade Offer encounter kind repurposed: presents a few *distinct roster items* (or a skip) and opens a Refit to place or swap the pick, retiring the old same-category auto-replace. Free. Now the **Offer** stage primitive (ADR-0014).
- **Shop** — the stage primitive presenting purchasable roster items; buying spends the ship's **cargo** — since ADR-0020 that is the wealth stowed in its holds, not a `starting_treasure` field (deleted) — and places the item through a Refit. A purchase makes the ship **lighter and heavier at once** (cargo leaves a hold, an item fills a slot); whether that reads as one action or two, and the exact spend-under-weight interaction, is left to the migration (fog). Stock is seed-baked; buying refills the shelf from the stock behind it and returns to the shop, so a visit can buy repeatedly until you **leave** (which completes the stage). Minimal economy: fixed starting budget, Offers free, shop purchases paid, no sell-back. Each buy within a visit costs more than the last (**cost escalation with purchase depth**, #124), so a shelf refills but never at the same price — and that surcharge is what the **shelf** window earns its keep on (ADR-0015): digging costs something only if buying reveals something new.
  Shop is **not Port-exclusive** — it is the **revealing** stage, so a Port is just the `[Shop]` recipe, while a merchant vessel at sea is an encounter that happens to carry a Shop stage. An encounter reveals iff its **first** stage reveals (ADR-0016), so a merchant — which always puts a stage in front of its Shop — stays **hidden**: a market you can plan a route to is what a Port is for, and a merchant is a windfall you meet. Its **cross-visit persistence is dropped** (ADR-0014 superseding ADR-0013): a Port resolves once like every other encounter, and `port_shelves` — the Sim's only per-node mutable voyage state — collapses. ADR-0013's persistent per-Port deck existed so a player could return for an item seen earlier, but a walked-once encounter has no second visit to return to.
  Shop is also the **one primitive that reads no stakes** (ADR-0015): it stocks as authored and prices by tier, because a Reward's payout is already site-scaled (#133), so a shop that improved with depth would compound the same progression from both ends. The market is fixed; the gradient is the cargo the captain brings to it.
- **Stock pool** — the Shop primitive's content roster (ADR-0015, #137): a named pool authoring **which** items a shop carries (a set of Tag families, or no filter at all) and **how deep** its hold is. Unlike every other roster it is **named by the recipe** rather than drawn, because a Port and a merchant vessel must not stock alike: a Port is *guaranteed* (two per zone), so routing to one is only worth planning if it is a dependable general market, while a merchant vessel is a *windfall* that can afford to be narrow. The Port's pool is the **Chandlery** — no filter, and deep enough that the shelf is still full when the starting cargo runs out. Specialist holds are one family and shallow enough to buy out, so the second purchase of a visit already bares a slot. Stocking "differently" means **subset and size**, deliberately not tier weighting (that is the stakes question, and Shop reads none) or price (economy tuning, and #124's surcharge is already the price knob).

### The expression language (see issue #393's spec)

- **Expression tree** — the arithmetic an Effect's magnitude is written in: a bounded, allocation-free, POD language of ten node kinds, evaluated by `expr_eval(tree, ctx)`. A tree is a **value** — an inline fixed array, no pool, no pointers, no allocation — so it costs a Ghost_Snapshot (ADR-0008) nothing beyond its bytes. Stored in **prefix order**, so arity implies structure and no child indices are stored. **Bound: 12 nodes per effect**, and no depth bound — depth cannot exceed node count, so a second rule would lock the same door twice. Since every arity is even, every well-formed tree has an odd node count, and the largest tree the bound admits is 11.
  _Avoid_: node pool, node ID — a tree is inline; scripted magnitude — the language is data, not code, and has **no RNG**.
- **Quantity** — the closed set a tree may read off the round: own Hull and Max Hull, own and opponent Speed, the round number, the **captain's own order**, the damage taken last round, and own visibility. The captain's order is **one ordinal** (`Hold=0, Press_Brace=1, Press_Fire=2, Commit=3`), never four flags — four would let "multiply by whether I pressed" and "select on whether I pressed" be one item at two prices — and being an encoding rather than a scale, only `Eq`/`Ne` compare it (rejected at authoring otherwise). Jettison and Break Off have no reading and land as Hold; the **opponent's order is unreadable**, because a scripted ship's order is a constant and carries no information. There is no `Count(empty slots)`: a vacated slot backfills a hold, so it was always the same fact as `count(Tag.Cargo)`.
- **Node kind** — the closed set of ten: `Const, Quantity, Count, Add, Sub, Mul, Min, Max, Pct, Gate`. **No `Div`** — a subtree divisor can be zero, and truncation makes it non-associative, so calibration would land on a rounding artifact; `Pct` replaces it with the divisor pinned at a literal 100. **No `Clamp` / `Sat_Sub` / `Abs` / `Sign`** — each saves a node or two and costs a permanent calibration row plus a second spelling of one intent.
- **Gate** — the language's only conditional and its only comparison, arity 4 (`op` on the node; `lhs, rhs, then, else` as children). **There are no booleans**: a comparison is consumed where it is made and never becomes a value, so "multiply by a comparison" and "select on a comparison" cannot be one item at two prices. `and` is nesting; `or` costs a duplicated branch. Gate is therefore the sole hook for the pricing model's conditionality discount.
- **Expr_Context** — everything a tree may read, flattened to plain data: a scalar per **Quantity**, a pre-counted census a **Count** selects over, and the opponent's (the **scouting report**). The evaluator takes **no Ship, no layout and no Battle**, which is what lets a ten-kind language be tested as arithmetic rather than through a battle. Who fills the context is the resolution wiring's business, not the evaluator's.
- **Authoring helper** — the `expr_const` / `expr_add` / `expr_gate` family, the only way a tree is written. Arity lives in the helper's signature, so a malformed tree is a **compile error** rather than a runtime surprise, and the node bound is spent (and asserted) as the tree is built.

## Vertical-slice scope (see GitHub issue #4)

- One fixed ship template: 8 slots (issue #91, ADR-0012) — Large x2, Medium x3, Small x3, split 4 exposed ("top deck", "top crew", "gun deck", "forecastle") / 4 concealed (one medium plus three small holds).
- Fixed starting loadout: "Top Crew", "Captain's Quarters", "Gun Deck" fill three exposed slots; a **hold** sized to its slot fills every remaining one, and the starting cargo water-fills across them (issue #91, #399).
- Findable content is limited to upgraded variants of those same three fittings (e.g. "Upgraded Gun Deck") — no separate fitting roster. _(Superseded by the build-variance effort's ~50-item roster — ADR-0012, #97.)_
- Exactly one captain.
- Map: a procedurally-generated connected node graph, Start/home port → Coastal → Open Sea → The Deep → Haven, ~50 points (17/17/16 per zone) of which 44 are encounters (15/15/14) and 6 are ports (2 per zone). Encounters are dealt via a per-zone shuffled **recipe** bag, drawn from the zone's stage-count bucket (Coastal 1 / Open Sea 2 / Deep 3). Supersedes the original 12-point open map. See ADR-0009 and ADR-0014.
